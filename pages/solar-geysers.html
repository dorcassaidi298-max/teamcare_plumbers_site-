How Solar Geysers Work

A solar geyser system uses roof-mounted solar collector panels to absorb heat from the sun and transfer it to water stored in an insulated geyser tank. On a typical sunny Zimbabwean day, the system reaches full operating temperature by mid-morning and maintains hot water through the evening. A thermostatically controlled electric backup element ensures you always have hot water even during extended cloudy periods — giving you complete peace of mind.

FAQs — Solar Geysers

How much can I save with a solar geyser?

In most Zimbabwean homes, the electric geyser accounts for 30–50% of the monthly electricity bill. A solar geyser can eliminate up to 70–80% of that cost, with most systems paying for themselves within 2–4 years through savings alone.

What happens on cloudy days or during power cuts?

Solar geyser systems are designed to retain heat for 24–48 hours in a well-insulated tank. An integrated electric backup element (powered by mains or a backup inverter) ensures continuous hot water availability even during extended overcast periods.

What size solar geyser do I need?

System sizing depends on the number of people in your household and your daily hot water usage. As a general guide: 100–150L for 1–2 people, 150–200L for 3–4 people, and 200–300L+ for larger families or commercial use. Our team will assess your specific requirements and recommend the right system.

How long does installation take?

A typical residential solar geyser installation is completed in one day. This includes mounting the collector panels on the roof, connecting the storage tank, running all pipework, and commissioning the system.

Do you offer warranties and after-sales service?

Yes. All solar geyser systems we supply come with manufacturer warranties (typically 5 years on the collector and 2–3 years on the tank). TeamCare also provides workmanship guarantees and ongoing service and maintenance contracts.
